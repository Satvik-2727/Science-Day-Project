    URANUS
    ATMOSPHERE
    Uranus rotates on its side, with an axial tilt of about 98 degrees. This extreme tilt causes its poles to receive more prolonged periods of sunlight or darkness, leading to unique seasonal variations and atmospheric effects.
    GEOLOGY
    Uranus has a system of faint rings composed mainly of dust, rock, and ice particles. These rings, though not as prominent as Saturn's, contribute to the planet's geophysical interactions.
    COMPOSITION
    Uranus is classified as an ice giant rather than a gas giant like Jupiter or Saturn due to its higher proportion of water, ammonia, and methane ices compared to the more hydrogen-rich gas giants.
