PLUTO A special planet. planet?
ATMOSPHERE Pluto's atmosphere is thin, composed mainly of nitrogen, with traces of methane and carbon monoxide. It freezes as Pluto moves away from the Sun, forming a surface ice layer. During its closest approach to the Sun, this ice sublimates, creating a temporary atmosphere. Despite being incredibly thin, it can expand due to seasonal variations, with gases escaping into space. The atmosphere freezes and collapses as Pluto moves farther from the Sun. Its dynamic nature, influenced by Pluto's elliptical orbit, results in a changing and transient atmosphere.

Pluto's atmosphere undergoes seasonal changes due to its elliptical orbit and axial tilt, similar to Earth. However, the seasons on Pluto are incredibly long, with each season lasting for several decades due to its long orbital period (about 248 Earth years).

COMPOSITION Pluto's composition primarily comprises rock and ice, with a mantle of water ice covering a rocky core. Its surface features a mix of frozen nitrogen, methane, and carbon monoxide. Organic compounds like tholins, formed by solar radiation interacting with methane and nitrogen, color its surface. Trace amounts of carbon dioxide and other hydrocarbons exist. Recent findings suggest the potential presence of an underground ocean beneath its icy shell. This diverse blend of rock, ice, and complex compounds defines Pluto's intriguing and varied composition.
