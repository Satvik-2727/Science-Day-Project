    MARS
    ATMOSPHERE
    Mars has a thin atmosphere composed mainly of carbon dioxide (95%) with traces of other gases. Its atmospheric pressure is about 1% that of Earth's, making it inhospitable for human life without protective equipment.
    GEOLOGY
    This vast system of canyons on Mars, named Valles Marineris, stretches over 2,500 miles (4,000 kilometers) long, making it one of the largest canyons in the solar system. It's believed to have formed from tectonic and erosional processes.
    COMPOSITION
    The presence of certain minerals and the detection of water ice suggest that Mars might have had conditions suitable for life in the past or could potentially support microbial life in subsurface environments.
