EARTH A miracle above science
ATMOSPHERE The Earth's atmosphere is a dynamic blanket of gases crucial for life. Comprising nitrogen,oxygen, and trace gases like carbon dioxide, it shields us from harmful solar radiation while regulating temperature. Divided into layers— troposphere, stratosphere, mesosphere, thermosphere, and exosphere—it facilitates weather patterns, sustains life, and enables vital processes like photosynthesis. Human activities impact its composition, leading to concerns like climate change and pollution, emphasizing the need for sustainable practices to preserve this delicate balance.
FACT FILE
The weight of the air above us creates atmospheric pressure. At sea level, the average atmospheric pressure is about 14.7 pounds per square inch (psi) or 101.3 kilopascals (kPa)
GEOLOGY Earth's geology spans four main layers: the solid inner core, molten outer core, mantle, and crust. Tectonic plates atop the semi-fluid mantle cause geological activity, forming mountains, valleys, and earthquakes at plate boundaries. Rock types-igneous, sedimentary, and metamorphic—reflect Earth's dynamic history through processes like volcanic eruptions, erosion, and pressure-induced changes. Fossil records within rock layers unveil evolutionary history, while minerals, such as quartz and feldspar, signify Earth's composition. Geological time, measured in eons, eras, and periods, illustrates the planet's ancient transformations and ongoing changes shaping its surface.
FACT FILE
Earth's surface is constantly changing due to plate tectonics, where the Earth's lithosphere (the outer layer of the Earth) is divided into several large and small plates that move and interact, causing earthquakes, volcanic eruptions, and the formation of mountains.
COMPOSITION Earth consists of distinct layers: the solid inner core, composed mainly of iron and nickel; the liquid outer core, also rich in iron and nickel; the semi-fluid mantle, containing silicate rocks and minerals; and the outermost layer, the crust, made up of lighter rocks like granite (continental crust) and basalt (oceanic crust). Elements like oxygen, silicon, aluminum, iron, calcium, sodium, and potassium constitute the majority, while trace elements contribute to Earth's diverse composition, fostering life and geological processes across its surface.
FACT FILE
The composition and movement of the iron-nickel core generate Earth's magnetic field. This magnetic field helps protect the planet from the solar wind and cosmic radiation.
